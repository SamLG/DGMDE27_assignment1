Winter 2015-2016.

Last Winter Was Dissapointing

 Last year's winter was abominable. Pardon the pun, but the winter was disappointing for anyone who isn't a snowbird or has aspirations of being a snowbird (snowbird \ˈsnō·bərd\ noun: an individual who annually migrates to warmer climates with the advent of cold weather). The snowbirds should have been happy with last winter, because we had the record warmest winter in over a century. It wasn't just that that winter isn't as dreary when it looks like a snowglobe, but the Vermont economy and ecosystem relies heavily on a good strong winter.

 The ski resorts and other winter activity businesses suffered great financial losses. Some ski resorts never opened, many others closed early. Only the resorts with snow machines were able to have any amount of adequate snow on the mountains, but making snow is expensive. I had a season's pass to a local ski resort, but only one run was open. Skiing the same slope for an entire day gets quite dull.

 The moose population suffered, as well as deer. Since the temperatures were so mild, the tick population boomed and some moose were literally sucked to death by the number of ticks on them. Vermont had to limit hunting permits on moose to help the population, although considering the level of damage no permits should have been allowed at all.
